Smart Shower

Design Requirements

  • Shower must have a way to control the water temperature minutely
  • Shower must have a way to control the water pressure minutely
  • Shower must have a way to control the nozzle (in what shape or pattern the water comes out)
  • Shower must support different users (preferences and presets)
  • Shower must have a way for a user to view usage data, including but not limited to volume, temperature, and pressure.
    Data should be seperable by individual user.

Assumptions

  • Shower has sensors in tap for temperature and volume
  • Shower contains a small 9:16 screen connected to a low voltage power supply
  • Showerhead has at least 3 nozzles and a motor that allows mechanical control between nozzles

Interviewing

Questions

  • What are some struggles you face when using a shower?
  • How much time do you spend in the shower?
  • What kind of information would be useful when it comes to showers?

Summary of responses

Generally, interviewees hate the experience of inconsistent water pressure and temperature. They spend between 10-15 minutes per day showering. Most interviewees feel that information on water usage would be beneficial.
